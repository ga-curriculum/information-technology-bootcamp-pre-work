At General Assembly, we believe timely *constructive feedback* delivered with empathy, respect, and clarity is essential to growth and learning. Constructive feedback should be given without fear of retribution and received with an open mind and a growth mindset.

> 📚 *Constructive feedback* is feedback that is fact-based, specific, and actionable. It should help the recipient improve rather than criticize or tear them down. It is focused purely on behaviors, actions, or work rather than personal aspects and provides clear guidance for growth.

## The value of fearless feedback

Without constructive feedback, it isn't easy to know how you're doing. We know the stakes are high in a bootcamp, and it's vital that you're able to assess your progress and make quick adjustments.

A culture of fearless feedback promotes continuous improvement, fosters collaboration, builds trust, and accelerates learning. It's a key component of a growth mindset, which is essential for success in a bootcamp environment.

Promote a feedback culture by seeking feedback from your peers and instructors when appropriate. Be open to receiving feedback, and use it to reflect on your work and improve.

> 🧠 Feedback should be positive too. When someone is doing great work, tell them! Simply recognizing of the value of another's work can make them feel appreciated and valued.

## Receiving feedback

When receiving feedback verbally, it's essential to engage in active listening. This allows you to understand and absorb the insights being shared with you fully. Active listening will enhance your understanding of the feedback and demonstrate to the person giving it that you value their input. It also helps you avoid misunderstandings and misinterpretations.

If it's necessary, ask clarifying questions or paraphrase the feedback to ensure you fully understand it. This will help you make the most of the feedback you receive.

> ⚠️ It's important not to take constructive feedback as a personal attack. Avoid becoming defensive or dismissive.
>
> If you initially disagree with the feedback, take some time to reflect and contemplate it. If you take the time to consider the input and still strongly disagree, consider seeking a second opinion from a knowledgeable source that you trust to be open and honest with you.

Fostering a feedback culture requires a commitment from everyone in the learning community. It's not just about receiving feedback; it's also about giving feedback.

## Giving feedback

When you give feedback, keep the points we've discussed in mind. Keep your feedback fact-based, specific, and actionable. Deliver your feedback with respect. Remember that the goal is to help the recipient improve, not to criticize or tear them down. Never focus on personal aspects when giving feedback.

Beyond the above, consider these tips when giving feedback:

- Use simple, direct, and brief language when giving feedback.
- Ask the recipient if they're open to receiving feedback before giving it.
- Ask the recipient how they would like to receive feedback - some people prefer written feedback, while others prefer verbal feedback.
- There is a time and place for feedback. You should give feedback privately and not in front of others.
- Feedback should be timely. Avoid waiting more than a few days to give feedback if it's tied to a specific event.

> ⚠️ Unless you've been placed into a group by your instructors, it's typically not your responsibility to provide constructive feedback directly to your peers. While we don't prevent you from doing so, you should understand that some may not feel comfortable receiving feedback from their peers without asking for it first, especially if they don't already have a rapport with you.

### Glows and grows

A common method for giving feedback is to provide both *glows* and *grows*. *Glows* are positive aspects of the work, while *grows* are areas that could be improved. This helps balance the feedback and ensures the recipient receives positive reinforcement and constructive criticism. This will help the recipient understand what they're doing well and what they can improve upon.

It also helps to ensure that the recipient doesn't feel overwhelmed by overly negative feedback. By providing positive feedback, you can help build the recipient's confidence and encourage them to continue to grow and improve.
