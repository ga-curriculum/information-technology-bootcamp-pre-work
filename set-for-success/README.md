## What can I do to succeed?

You should be feeling motivated after that reflection exercise. Use that energy to commit to these strategies that will help you succeed in ITB:

### Complete the rest of this pre-work

This pre-work is designed to help you prepare for the course. You're required to complete this work before the first day of class.

### Be your own advocate

Be present and engaged in class. Ask questions when you have them, participate in discussions, and collaborate with your peers. When something is wrong or you need help, speak up!

<blockquote class="warning">
  🚨 We can't help you if we don't know there is a problem!
</blockquote>

### Help others

Share in someone else's struggles and celebrate their success together. You'll learn more when you teach others and build a strong network of peers who can support you when you need it along the way.

### Have patience with yourself

Learning is a process. Embrace the challenges and allow yourself time to grow. Some concepts will come quickly to you, and others will take more time.

Try not to compare yourself to others. Everyone comes into the course with different backgrounds and experiences. Be your own benchmark and focus on making yourself 1% better than you were yesterday.

### Limit your obligations

This course is intense, and you'll need to dedicate a significant amount of time to it. Try to limit other obligations during the course so you can focus on your learning. Let your friends and family know you'll need to devote a much of your time to your studies over the next few months.

### Stay healthy

Take care of yourself during the course by protecting your sleep schedule and eating well. It's easy to let these things slide when you're busy, but they're essential for your well-being and your ability to learn.

Keep tabs on your mental health as well. Ensure you're making time for activities that help you manage stress, relax, and recharge. Physical activity, meditation, and hobbies can all be helpful. Don't forget to take breaks - use timers to help you remember to step away from your work.

> ⚠️ Your instructors are not trained mental health professionals. They're glad to listen to what is on your mind and can share resources, but remember that they have professional boundaries. Ensure you have a support system in place to help you manage your mental health.

### Keep a steady pace

The bootcamp is a marathon, not a sprint. Maintaining a steady pace throughout the course is essential to avoid burnout. Don't wait until the last minute to complete assignments or study for exams. Break your work into manageable chunks and work on it consistently.

It can be hard to get back on track if you start to put things off.

### Check the hardware requirements

Ensure your computer meets the [technical requirements](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1dcmz023ZTQsuz9FCky_e2LGPgrCKpsooVCr1Xlrbpxg/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.1dzat4n5dyq5) for the course. Update your operating system if it's out of date.

<div class="activity solo-exercise">
  <h2 class="title">Think about how you'll stay organized</h2>
  <span class="minutes">10 min</span>
</div>

Spend a few minutes identifying the tools and systems that work best for you to stay organized. Think about how you will:

- Keep track of assignments, due dates, and other responsibilities.

- Take notes during lectures and while studying.

  > 💡 Lean into your learning style - some people benefit from taking notes in lectures, others don't. If taking notes is not a good learning strategy for you, don't feel like you have to!

- Keep your physical space organized and distraction-free. Consider how to prevent spills and other accidents that could damage your computer.

- How will you keep track of digital references and resources? This could be anything from organizing bookmarks in your browser to using a note-taking app.
